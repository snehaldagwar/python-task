Defination of Deep Learing

Deep learning is a subset of machine learning driven by multilayered artificial neural networks. Inspired by the human brain, it processes raw, unstructured data to autonomously uncover patterns, make predictions, and solve complex problems without requiring explicit human programming.

How it Works


Deep learning is called "deep" because it uses networks with many layers (often dozens or hundreds) of interconnected nodes, or "neurons".
